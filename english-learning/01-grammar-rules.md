# ЁЯУЭ Grammar Rules тАУ Learned from Practice

These rules were identified from real sentence corrections in professional IT communication.

---

## 1. Use Direct Verbs (Remove "would")

тЭМ `we would require your support`  
тЬЕ `we require your support`

**Rule:** "Would" makes sentences sound uncertain. In professional requests, use direct present tense unless asking for a favour politely.

---

## 2. Correct Verb + Participle Order

тЭМ `we have already been mitigated this`  
тЬЕ `we have already mitigated this`

тЭМ `we have security patching been applied`  
тЬЕ `security patching has been applied`

**Rule:** In perfect tense, verb order is: **have/has + past participle**. Do not insert extra words between them.

---

## 3. Use the Article "the" Correctly

тЭМ `please find attached OEM errata fix`  
тЬЕ `please find attached the OEM errata fix`

тЭМ `rest of team`  
тЬЕ `the rest of the team`

**Rule:** Use "the" before a specific noun that both the speaker and reader know about.

---

## 4. Remove Redundant Words

тЭМ `Any anomalies (if any) detected`  
тЬЕ `Any anomalies detected`

тЭМ `in an urgent case` (after already writing "Urgent:")  
тЬЕ Remove it тАФ the label already says Urgent

**Rule:** If the meaning is already clear from context, do not repeat it.

---

## 5. Fix "also" Spelling

тЭМ `We have alos planned`  
тЬЕ `We have also planned`

**Rule:** Common typo тАФ "alos" тЖТ "also". Slow down when typing conjunctions.

---

## 6. Tense Consistency in Formal Updates

тЭМ `observations identified would be documented` (mixing future intent and past observation)  
тЬЕ `observations identified will be documented`

**Rule:** Use "will" for future commitments in professional updates, not "would."

---

## 7. Proper Noun Capitalization

тЭМ `openshift`, `openssl`, `redis operator`  
тЬЕ `OpenShift`, `OpenSSL`, `Redis Operator`

**Rule:** Product names, technologies, and acronyms must always be capitalized correctly.

---

## 8. Version Formatting

тЭМ `V-4-19`, `V-4.19`  
тЬЕ `v4.19`

**Rule:** Use lowercase `v` followed directly by the version number. No hyphens between major/minor.

---

## 9. Hyphen vs Em Dash Usage

тЭМ `N-1- version` (extra hyphen)  
тЬЕ `N-1 version`

**Rule:** Hyphens join compound modifiers. Do not add extra hyphens at the end of a word.

---

## 10. Plural Agreement

тЭМ `installed operators components`  
тЬЕ `installed operator components`

**Rule:** When a noun modifies another noun (compound noun), the modifier stays singular: `operator components`, not `operators components`.

---

## 11. Add "to be" for State Descriptions

тЭМ `All cluster nodes were confirmed in Ready state`  
тЬЕ `All cluster nodes were confirmed to be in a Ready state`

**Rule:** When confirming a state, use **confirmed + to be + in + [state]**.
